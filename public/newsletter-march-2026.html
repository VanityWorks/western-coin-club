Opening Note

A Movement Begins

When Thomas van der Spuy set out to launch the South African Coin Collectors Club, the vision was simple - yet ambitious: to build a community that would elevate the hobby, connect collectors, and give numismatics a stronger, unified voice in South Africa.

This was not a new idea.

For many years, Thomas had been considering this concept, discussing it with prominent numismatists and dealers across the country. The foundation had been forming quietly in the background. Then, following key events in early March, the decision was made - the time is now.

And with that, the South African Coin Collectors Club was born.

What followed exceeded all expectations.

The Club began with a soft launch, welcoming its first members, before officially launching at the 2026 Johannesburg Coin Show - where 131 paid members joined on the day. By the end of March, that number had grown to 383 members, making it the largest community of coin collectors in South Africa.

But this was no coincidence.

It was the result of genuine passion, careful planning, the right team of dedicated collectors - and most importantly, a supportive community that was ready for something more.

Ready to connect.
Ready to grow.
Ready to build the future of the hobby together.

And this is only the beginning.

Coin of the Month

2026 JHB Coin Show Privy Mark Krugerrand

1 oz Fine Silver · R1

To commemorate the 2026 Johannesburg Coin Show, the South African Mint authorized a special privy mark release of the 1 oz Silver Krugerrand. This coin represents both a collectible numismatic item and a historic marker of March 2026 - the month the hobby transformed.

The authorization to strike 1,000 coins came from the SA Mint's desire to give collectors a tangible piece of the show's legacy. However, collector demand resulted in the delivery of only 500 coins before stock was exhausted. The remaining 500 coins from the authorized mintage may or may not be released at the Mint's discretion.

This scarcity dynamic - authorized vs. delivered - has become a critical factor in pricing. Experienced collectors understand that "authorized mintage" and "actual delivery" are two different things. The 500 coins delivered represent the true scarcity; the additional 1,000 authorized represent potential future supply of another 500.

Education Corner: AU vs MS (Understanding the Difference)

The difference between About Uncirculated (AU) and Mint State (MS) remains one of the most important concepts in coin grading.

Under the internationally accepted 70-point Sheldon scale, a coin graded AU58 may display only the slightest trace of wear on the highest points of the design, while still retaining nearly full detail and much of its original lustre. In contrast, a coin graded MS60 or MS61 shows no wear at all, but may exhibit numerous marks, abrasions, or weak strike characteristics.

In simple terms:

AU = slight wear, but often visually attractive

MS = no wear, but can still look "worse" due to marks

Old British Grading Perspective

In the old British grading system, the interpretation differs slightly:

AU58 typically aligns closer to EF (Extremely Fine)

EF coins are described as having light wear with strong detail

Uncirculated (UNC) is reserved strictly for coins with no wear, even if marks are present

Hearn's Catalogue Insight

In Hearn's catalogue (based on the Old British grading system):

An AU58 coin is often valued closer to EF pricing, not fully Uncirculated

This reflects the traditional emphasis on actual wear over surface marks

For more accurate pricing interpretation, collectors are encouraged to study page 4 of the catalogue carefully.

Why Grading Matters

Third-party grading has become a cornerstone of modern numismatics. It allows collectors to buy and sell coins with far greater confidence - especially in an increasingly digital marketplace.

By assigning an independent, standardised grade, professional grading services provide a common language for condition, making it easier to compare coins, establish value, and support market liquidity. This is particularly important when a buyer cannot physically inspect a coin before purchasing.

As the hobby continues to move online, trusted and consistent grading is no longer just helpful - it is essential for transparency, trust, and efficient trading.

Protection for the Future

Beyond grading, encapsulation (slabbing) plays a crucial role in preserving coins for future generations.

Coins sealed in tamper-evident holders are protected from scratches, handling damage, environmental exposure, and even accidental drops, ensuring their condition remains unchanged over time.

In this way, grading is not only about value - it is about long-term preservation, allowing coins to be safely passed down and enjoyed by future generations.

Key Takeaway

Don't just collect - learn to grade. Pick up a coin, study its high points under a loupe, and compare it against reference images. Over time, your eye will sharpen, and your collection will reflect it.

⚠ SCAM ALERT: Mandela R5 and Social Media Coin Scams

Mandela R5 coins and other run-of-the-mill circulation coins are being advertised across social media as being worth hundreds of thousands - if not millions - of rands. They are not.

The Scheme: Scammers request a "processing fee" upfront for onboarding, KYC and documentation - typically R500–R1,500 - claiming it covers grading verification, insurance, or authentication.

Red Flags: If a seller demands payment before delivery, or requests fees via wire transfer or gift cards, it is a scam. Legitimate dealers do not operate this way.

What To Do: Comment "Scam" on suspicious Facebook posts and report the post - it literally takes a few seconds. Encourage others to do the same. Small actions repeated by many collectors will make a real difference with the social media algorithms.

Talk of the Hobby

Ending March with the Talk of the Hobby

The coin is invisible - and most bidders saw nothing.
Yet, its meaning is not.
Perhaps that is the point.

Where many could not see beyond the concept, one buyer may have seen what others did not - or could not. Not the object itself, but the bigger picture. The story. The significance. The opportunity.

That buyer acted.

The most talked-about lot in South African numismatics this month has to be Lot 15 from Signature Auction 23 on the Bassani's Coin Auction App - The Invisible Coin - which realised an extraordinary R66,720 (including buyer's premium).

A record? Very likely. The most expensive slab ever sold in South Africa? Possibly. A recognised South African rarity - almost overnight? Without a doubt.

What began as a light-hearted April Fool's concept - the idea of an "invisible coin" - quickly evolved into something far more significant. It captured attention, sparked debate, and ultimately commanded real conviction in the market.

But this moment may represent something deeper.

We are seeing this across the world in contemporary art - where narrative, concept, and cultural meaning elevate an object far beyond its physical form. In that same way, The Invisible Coin may mark a shift in South African numismatics - where story and significance begin to stand alongside metal and mintage.

After all, the coin is invisible - yet its meaning is not.

And that vision may, in time, prove to be a stroke of genius - not simply a purchase, but a statement. A belief in narrative. A belief in the future. A belief that rarity can be created as much as it is discovered.

We may well have witnessed the birth of a modern South African numismatic legend.

There will only ever be one Invisible Coin.

Only time will tell what the market ultimately holds - but its place in South African numismatics, and in the conversations shaping its future, is already secured.

And if history has shown us anything, it is that the pieces that challenge convention are often the ones that matter most. This may, in time, prove to be one of them.

What makes this moment even more significant is what followed. In an extraordinary gesture, Dario and the team at SANGS donated 100% of the proceeds to the South African Coin Collectors Club - directly supporting the growth, development, and future of the hobby.

We extend our sincere thanks to SANGS for their belief in this movement. As a community, we will make you proud.
